Title: The Effect of Procrastination on Physical Exercise among College Students—The Chain Effect of Exercise Commitment and Action Control
Authors: Wenjuan Zhang, Menglin Xu, Yujuan Feng, Zhixiong Mao, Zengyin Yan
Year: 2024
Keywords: college, procrastination, exercise, commitment, action

Abstract:
Exercise procrastination is prevalent among college students, causing a decline in physical fitness. This study investigates the effect of procrastination on physical exercise behavior, along with the chain mediation effects of exercise commitment and action control. A questionnaire-based study was conducted on 581 college students using validated scales and statistical models. Results show that procrastination negatively affects exercise behavior, while exercise commitment and action control fully mediate this relationship. The chain mediation effect also plays a significant role, indicating that improving commitment and action control can reduce procrastination and enhance physical activity among students.
